What is Citizenship?
Being a citizen means that you belong to a country. Citizens have rights, like the right to vote, and responsibilities, like following the country's laws. In India, Part II of the Constitution explains who is an Indian citizen.

üìù Detailed Breakdown of Articles:

Article 5

At the time India became a republic (January 26, 1950), anyone who lived in India, was born in India, or whose parents were born in India would be an Indian citizen.

Article 6

After independence, some people moved from Pakistan to India. If they moved before July 19, 1948, they automatically became citizens. After that, they needed government permission.

Article 7

This article explains that people who moved to Pakistan but returned to India could still be citizens if the government allowed it.

Article 8

People of Indian origin living outside India, whose parents or grandparents were born in India, could apply to be citizens.

Article 9

India does not allow dual citizenship. If you become a citizen of another country, you lose Indian citizenship.
